Vision

We shall establish for all humans worldwide, an easy to use, free of cost, state of the art, standards compliant, open sourced, censorship resistant, operating system and decentralized communication infrastructure. We shall evolve this platform for personal, public or private socialization, trade, finance, journalism, art, science, research, education and community self-governance.

Mission

We must collaborate transparently to promote merit, equity and inclusion in our activities. We endeavor to enhance society’s infrastructure, empower individuals, families and communities to prosper free from industry overlords and to thrive in a fair and accessible manner by expanding the user base of decentralized, open source technologies. Together, we shall continuously improve our system architecture, network engineering, and software development processes in ways that protect even the lowliest individual while strengthening self-reliance and sovereignty, family unity, and encouraging communities to self-organize.

Licence

BSD/MIT

All work for this society shall be released with included source code and all of the allowances granted under the BSD and MIT licenses.

The Berkeley Standard Distribution Licence and Massachusetts Institute of Technology License are among the most concise and permissive licensing agreements widely recognized by governments and the industries of technology and software development, drafted to maximally preserve the rights of users along with the liberties of engineers and developers. Contrast these to so-called “copyleft” licenses such as GNU/GPL (Gnu’s Not Unix, Gnu Public License) which attempt to enforce a socialist contract among their developers and users that, while certainly well-intending, trends toward monopolicy, oligopoly and nexuses of power naturally arising out of that misguided (if nonetheless noble) ideology while simultaneously hamstringing their users’ and adopters’ means of escape from beneath the “umbrella”.

Same.Name

Current proprietary tech monopolies are maintained, and fledgling market entrants kept divided, through their necessary ownership of each’s pertinent and fractured aspects of users’ identities and reputations. Namecoin (the first and most well established invention ever to effectively solve the ageless engineering trilemma known as Zooko’s triangle) empowers individuals to have total sovereignty over their identity with no centralized entity of authority nor any single point of failure. Free and open sourced nuOS software builds upon Namecoin to enable private and decentralized individual management of societal concepts such as association, reputation, copyright, trademark, patent and much more, all in a boundless and permissionless global network, remaining backward compatible while exponentially increasing Namecoin’s robustness against Sybil attack.

The branded service Same.Name is offered to lower the barriers of entry into this innovative network. Using Same.Name, vanilla nuOS or any other compatible software implementation of their choosing, each participant may take individual responsibility for subject/object authorization while the greater global network seamlessly provides subject authentication and object integrity. This allows every user to take back ownership of their identity and reputation while surfing freely between sites and apps without logging in or revealing any sensitive or personally identifiable information whatsoever. This conjunction technologies allows users the flexibility to migrate memberships and distribute data entirely at their own will without losing relationships between subjects or links between objects, all while eliminating would-be permission-granting or limiting authorities. Thus, new entrants to an entirely new landscape of social technologies are empowered to innovate and flourish on stable and even footing, to compete with established behemoths primarily on the basis of service quality and value rather than predominantly by their size and power.
